Assignment 6.1: Individual Research

The goal for this exercise for me to choose a topic that has to do with Language Awareness and research it, after which i will present my findings.

A particular point in linguistics that interested me was the speed in which languages are spoken. The speed at which a language is spoken is something near impossible to accurately measure as there is no standard that it can be held to. There are multiple factors within measuring that might also throw off calculations such as the pauses an individual might take in their sentences and the amount of syllables in the language itself. Studies for measuring
Because measuring the language was this difficult, studies often used two kinds of methods to measure the speed of a language. One of them was the speaking rate which included pauses and hesitation and the other method without taking pauses into account. Two commonly used terms in this research were speaking rate and articulation rate. Both being heavily corelated with speech tempo. Speaking rate was the rate in which sounds left the mouth of the speaker, which would normally include pauses in speech. The other method, articulation rate, measured the speed at which fully articulated words left the mouth of the speaker to measure it per word, this method does not measure pauses, as it measures the amount of words, nothing between these words. To effectively compare languages, rules were put in place to penalize individuals who talk too quickly and frequently make mistake, these rules would ensure that individuals would not try to speak out of their language’s speed intentionally. Comparing languages
When comparing measurements between languages, the scientists encountered another problem, the amount of syllables in a word. For example, if syllables were measured Finnish would be spoken more quickly than English, yet if words were to be measured the English language would come out on top. To make it even more difficult, the measurement method that uses words can be thrown off on basis of how a “word” is defined. In an attempt to accurately measure the speaking speed of a language, the syllables-per-second measurement was created. This method might not be that much more efficient in measuring a language’s speed, it should be kept in mind that different languages have very different syllable structures. Many of the world’s languages do not use syllables with more than two or three sounds, while other languages might allow drastically more sounds per syllable. A language like The Japanese language is able to fit more syllables into a given timeframe than a language with a more complicated language structure, like the language structure of the English language.
