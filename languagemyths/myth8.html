Language Myth 8

Children can't speak or write properly anymore

For centuries, there have been individuals that recurrently complain about the current state of the English language, these individuals believe the language is in decline mainly because of how the younger generation mistreats the language structure, the main reason for this being improper education. These individuals often reference a kind of “golden age” in which grammatical knowledge would have been far more developed than it is now. This situation, however, has never proven to having taken place. In fact, the proficiency in the English language was extremely poor until the 1944 education act which only taught enough vocabulary skill to write simple notes. A lot of these individuals believe that mandatory grammatical test will serve in keeping the “quality” of the language. These methods have not been proven to work and even been said to have the opposite effects for people who learn the language at a slower pace. All in all, I do think a standard of English should be kept, as that is necessary for the grammatical rules to stand, though I do not think mandatory tests will be effective. A language can keep up a standard, but still have the ability to evolve itself.
