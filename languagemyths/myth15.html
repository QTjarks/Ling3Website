Language Myth 15

TV makes people sound the same

Sociolinguists often find themselves discussing changes in the speech communities around them, most of them being fairly apparent like the replacement of a certain term within a language. These changes in the language will undoubtedly happen, though the reason for these changes is often not apparent. The reasons could range from grammatical finetuning to young adults becoming more active on the job marketplace, which makes deducing the motivation of the change that much more difficult. People often look toward the media when discussing the topic, considering the media does have the widest range of all other mediums. In their theory, the media commissions new words and uses them regularly so people who listen in can pick it up and start using it. This has been proven to be faulty however, even though the media does have an extensive range of listeners, the media does not actually have any major impact on the language. I do think the media plays some part in changing the language, even though it may not be a major one. And the news should pay close attention to the terminology they choose to use toward their audience.
