Language Myth 13

“Black children are verbally deprived” By Walt Wolfram

Verbal depreciation has been a prominent topic within the POC community, it has become ironic to describe African-American children with terms such as “verbally deprived” or “linguistically retarded” while some of the most powerful and often quoted speeches come from people with the same roots.

Before beginning to dissect this argument, the fact that there are varieties in the English language which may from time-to-time correlate with ethnicity. The root of the issue would be in the theme of “the linguistic inferiority principle” according to this principle, the speech of the socially subordinate group will always be interpreted as inadequate when compared to the socially dominant type of English.

Thus, when African American speech is compared to middle class English, the African American speech will appear to be linguistically deficient. These arguments will from time to time be made towards the African American type of English when in reality, that type of speech is not as flawed as perceived. The speech used is grammatically correct and follows the necessary rules within the language.

I find that the language deficiency is not necessarily a point of deficiency, but one of forcing students to perceive a certain type of English as correct. Which is why I find language awareness important; it can provide a manner of learning for every variety of English.

I more than agree with Wolfram’s statement that: “Rather than being labelled as verbally deprived, African-Americans ought to be thanked for contributing to daily conversation with words, phrases and other manners of speaking that enrich our language and our lives.” (Page 111)
We should open up more to the idea of learning from each other and helping out more instead of conspiring over myths if you’d ask me.
