Language Myth 1

The meaning of words should not be allowed to vary or change.” By Peter Trudgill

“All languages change all the time. It is not very well understood why this is the case, but it is a universal characteristic of human languages.”(Page 1)[1].

This myth focusses on how the meaning of certain words can change over time and by cultures. The meaning of words in the English language have changed significantly over time and differ from dialect to dialect. In most cases, the chance of miscommunication is largely mitigated by reading the context of the sentence in question. Through this evolution of the language, it is shown that the meaning of a word is not truly decided by the definition specified in the dictionary, it is decided by what the majority perceives the meaning to be.

“A number of people seem to think that the fact that languages change the meaning of their words is unfortunate. They believe a change in language is inherently undesirable and that we should so everything we can to stop it because change can be dangerous and confusing.” (Page 3)[1].

The evolution of the English language is something that happens constantly and will continue to happen, at perhaps an even faster pace in the age of technology we live in. Language is ever evolving, and even though certain words may lose the meaning they previously had, I believe the evolution of language is necessary to keep up with the quickly evolving world we live in now.
